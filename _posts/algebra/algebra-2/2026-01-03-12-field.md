**Definition**
Let $K$ be an extension of a field $F$, and let $\alpha$ be an element of $K$. By analogy with the definition of algebraic numbers, $\alpha$ is **algebraic** over $F$ if it is a root of a monic polynomial with coefficients in $F$, say $$f(x) = x^n+a_{n-1} x^{n-1}+\cdots + a_0,$$ with $a_i$ in $F$ and $f(\alpha) = 0$. An element is **transcendental** if it is not algebraic over $F$.
:::

## field extensions

$F$ be a field.

1.  $F\to R$ every ring hom from $F$ to $R\ne 0$ is injective.

2.  the canonical map $\varphi:\mathbb{Z}\to F$. Consider $\ker \varphi\to \mathbb{Z}/\ker \varphi \hookrightarrow F$, then $\mathbb{Z}/\ker\varphi$ be integral domain $\iff$ $\ker \varphi$ prime ideal $=(0) or (p)$

**Definition**
A **field extension** $K/F$ means: $F\hookrightarrow K$. note $K$ is an $F$-vector space $\implies$ $[K:F]:=\dim_F K$ be **extension degree**. A field extension is finite of degree $n$ if its extension degree is $n$. Otherwise the extension is infinite. $F\hookrightarrow M\hookrightarrow K\to M$ intermediate field extension of $K/F$.
:::

**Proposition**

(1) $[K:F] = 1\iff K=F$.

(2) $K/M/F$, $[K:F]=[K:M][M:F]$.

## algebraic extension

**Lemma**
$\alpha,\beta\in K$ algebraic$/F$ $\implies$ $\alpha\pm \beta,\alpha\cdot\beta,1/\alpha,\beta/\alpha$ are algebraic$/F$
:::

**Definition**
Say $K/F$ is algebraic if $\forall \alpha\in K$ is algebraic in $F$.
:::

**Remark**
$[K:F]<\infty\implies K/F$ is algebraic.
